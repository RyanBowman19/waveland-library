About & History

Over a century of stories — and we're still writing them.

Beginnings

How a Literary Club Built a Library

In 1913, members of a literary group called The Priscilla Club began plans for a Carnegie library. The ladies felt they could meet the requirements: a need for a public library, a building site, 10% of the construction cost, funds for yearly support and operation, and a promise to provide free service to all.

Mary Myers Krit, Louise Hopkins Huston, Minnie Conner Shar, Caroline Granger Burrin, Minnie Cuppy Kritz, and Rhoda Hobson Fullenwider gathered the required signatures under the state library law for building.

Judge Jere West, Minnie Kritz, Louise Huston, and Dr. Arthur John Kleiser became the first trustees. John Dewitt Fisher and Mary Virginia Oldshue Glover were added by the town to make up the first five-member board of directors. After several changes — Irving Fullenwider and U.G. Vail joining, then Walter Penn taking Vail's place — the final board for the Waveland Carnegie Library was set.

In each of these founders' obituaries, we find proudly displayed their part in the founding of our impressive library.

The Building

The Carnegie Library Building

It's hard to imagine that such a large, beautiful brick building was erected for a mere $10,000. The basic structure and layout has changed little since 1915. Up the stairs to the east are the adult books, while the children's section is still to the west.

The name on a bronze tablet in the entryway reads “Gift from Andrew Carnegie.” Much of the original golden oak remains in the main room, and the first reading tables are still in use. World-renowned artist T.C. Steele (opens in new tab), one of Waveland's most famous sons, donated a painting in honor of the opening — it still hangs in its intended spot above the fireplace. View the painting → (opens in new tab)

The architect was William F. Sharpe of Crawfordsville; the builder was Charles Franklin Rice of Roachdale, one of the most prolific of his time. The downstairs has always had an extremely active community area — movies, story hours, and presentations have filled the large meeting room for eleven decades now, and fond memories abound.

Building Facts

  • Built: 1914–1915
  • Cost: $10,000
  • Brick: From Brazil, Clay County — $14 per thousand
  • Finishes: Sherwin-Williams paint & oak stain
  • Roof: Original slate, replaced in the 2014 remodel
  • Heat: Boiler heat with the original radiators

Fun Facts from Our First Century

861 people signed up by the opening festivities. The first five were all women: Grace and Minnie Kritz, Grace Pen, Sallie Penn Burroff, and Lydia Shanks.
In 1918, there was a major book drive for the soldier camps: “Have a good book? Donate it, and if you don't have one, buy them one!”
In 1933, author LeRoy MacLeod — born near Browns Valley — visited the library. His novel Three Steeples, based on his small town, was widely read locally.
In 1943, a magazine craze brought subscriptions to Parents' Magazine, Collier's Weekly, and Child Life.
Waveland and Brown Township citizens were given a free library card — a policy that remains today.
Names of early signers still found in the area: Bannon, Bayless, Canine, Durham, Fullenwider, Hallett, Lough, Machledt, Moser, Rice, and Wiatt.
Three local clubs donated $10 each for new books, and Current Events Club members each gave ten books. Waveland's collection was considered one of the largest and best in the state.
Early librarians never imagined today's media — computers, DVDs, audiobooks, and e-books — but librarians today still order lots of regular books!

Milestones

Celebrations

  • April 17, 1915 — Grand Opening Music by Orville and Williams Settle, the high school choir, and a piano solo by Myrtle Redidsh Oglesbee. Invocations by Roscoe Smith (Christian Church) and F.B. Solin (Presbyterian Church). Speeches by State Librarian Dr. Demarchus Brown (“The Library and Democracy”) and Crawfordsville School Superintendent Prof. L.N. Hines (“A Sign of Progress”). Gifts included a grandfather clock and a Japanese flower basket.
  • September 26, 2015 — Centennial Open house and refreshments, a concert by Danny Hopkins, Family & Friends, and an appearance by Mike McCarty, author of Choking in Fear.

Leadership

Board of Trustees

The Board of Trustees is appointed through the Southmont School Board, County Council, and County Commissioners. Board members serve four-year terms and may serve up to four terms. Board meetings are held in the library on the last Tuesday of every month at the time posted on our Events calendar.
